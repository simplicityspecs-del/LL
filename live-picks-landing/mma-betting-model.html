Methodology

The MMA betting model in plain English.

The Live Picks model is built for UFC head-to-head markets. It combines pre-fight probability, market price and late fight-night signals instead of trying to predict every round or method outcome.

Model scope Price, timing, condition and composure.

The goal is to find moments where the market is still leaning on paper while the live read has shifted.

Core idea

A model is not a crystal ball.

An MMA betting model should not pretend fights are clean spreadsheets. Style matchups, durability, cardio, pressure response and judging risk all matter before the event. Then fight night adds another layer: weigh-in aftermath, body language, crowd response, walkout pace and late market movement.

Live Picks MMA treats the model as a decision framework. It helps decide whether a head-to-head price is worth watching, then whether the late read is strong enough to lock.

The signal stack.

1Pre-fight baseStyle, recent form, durability, cardio and likely pace.
2Market checkOpening price, closing pressure and whether the odds still make sense.
3Fight-night readCondition, movement, composure, crowd reception and pressure signs.

Signals the model watches.

  • Condition: stiffness, fatigue markers, injury masking and whether the fighter looks physically sharp.
  • Movement: balance, bounce, posture, breathing rhythm and guarded movement before contact.
  • Composure: nerves, anger, distraction, over-excitement or emotional hijacking.
  • Reception: hostile crowd, silence, pressure, big boos or a reaction that changes the atmosphere.
  • Market lag: price that still appears anchored to hype, record, name value or old assumptions.

Why the record uses units.

The public record uses flat 1u staking to keep the analysis readable. One unit is one standard stake. That means a +15.48u record is up 15.48 standard stakes, regardless of whether someone personally thinks in $10, $100 or another amount.

Units make the result easier to compare without pretending everyone has the same bankroll.
