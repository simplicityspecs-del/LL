Delivery infrastructure

Why we use Substack.

Live Picks matters most right before the fight starts. Substack lets us send locks straight to subscriber inboxes and notifications instead of relying on refresh lists, private pages, or overloaded infrastructure.

The simple reason

Late picks need reliable delivery.

A normal article can load slowly. A fight-night lock cannot. Substack gives us a proven publishing, subscriber, email and notification layer for instant-demand moments.

Traffic is quiet. Then it spikes.

Most of the week is a trickle. On fight night, attention arrives all at once. Email and app notifications reduce refresh pressure at the exact moment speed matters.

What matters
Most of the week is calm

People read the thesis, check results, or wait for the next card.

Fight night compresses demand

Subscribers arrive together because the final read may not exist until the walkout.

Delivery speed is part of the product

A late lock needs to arrive through email and notifications, not constant refreshes.

Self-hosting risk

Looks fine until everyone arrives at once.

  • Traffic burstsFight-night surges can slow a lightweight setup at the exact moment a lock needs to go out.
  • Refresh loadLast-minute picks make users refresh more, adding pressure to private infrastructure.
  • Too many moving partsHosting, payments, logins and notifications become separate failure points.
Why Substack fits

Use proven rails and focus on the model.

  • Email and notification deliveryLocks go directly to subscribers instead of relying on a private page staying responsive.
  • Established infrastructureSubstack already handles subscribers, publishing, email and notifications at scale.
  • Cleaner focusWe focus on the live read and price discipline, not infrastructure firefighting.
Substack is not the product. The live read is.

Substack is the delivery layer. Live Picks is the model, the timing and the decision to release only when late fight-night information justifies it.
